Planeat: AI-powered meal planning app

How can we help people plan their daily meals in a healthy, efficient, and budget-friendly way?

The challenge was to design an app that uses Artificial Intelligence to create personalized meal plans and shopping lists, tailored to users' time, budget, and dietary preferences. The goal is to provide a comprehensive app that simplifies daily meal organization and reduces the stress around it, all with an intuitive and user-friendly design.

01. Discover, understand, and empathize

Understanding the challenge and research objectives

  • Researching user habits, frustrations, and priorities through surveys and interviews.
  • Identifying key pain points such as lack of time, tight budgets, and meal personalization through needs analysis.
  • Analyzing similar apps to uncover opportunities, such as integrating shopping lists with real-time pricing.
  • Exploring the potential of Artificial Intelligence to address meal planning challenges reported by users.

Primary Research

User interviews

Eight interviews were conducted with individuals aged 28 to 42 to explore their dietary needs, focusing on time constraints, budget concerns, healthy eating, and technology usage. These conversations revealed key habits, priorities, and frustrations related to meal planning, resource optimization, and recipe adaptation.

User surveys

Surveys confirmed the need for an app designed to address challenges such as lack of time and meal ideas (78%), budget optimization, and the integration of essential features. Additionally, most respondents (95%) already organize weekly meal plans or use shopping lists, while 65% use food-related apps several times a week, valuing access to recipes and meal planners (91%).

View user surveys

Key research findings

  • Users need an app that combines meal planning, shopping list creation, and recipe customization in one place to save time and simplify their daily routine.
  • Lack of time and ideas limits healthy meal planning, making personalized suggestions essential.
  • Optimizing food budgets is a priority, with users valuing features such as price comparison tools and budget-based meal planning.
  • The use of food-related apps is growing, highlighting an opportunity to integrate AI technology for an enhanced experience.

Problem statement

People with busy schedules and tight budgets struggle to maintain a healthy and cost-effective diet, often resorting to less nutritious or more expensive alternatives. Planeat provides an AI-powered solution that centralizes meal planning through a personalized recipe calendar and budget-optimized shopping lists, helping users save time and develop healthier eating habits.

03. Ideating the solution

How Might We

The first step in designing an MVP that addressed the objectives identified during the research phase began with an exploration of how we could solve the detected problems and challenges. To do this, the following "How Might We" questions were formulated:

Prioritization

Based on previous findings, I defined an MVP focused on automated meal planning, customization according to budget and dietary preferences, and a simple interface that enhances the user experience by making it easier and more practical.

View MoSCoW

Information Architecture

Planeat's architecture is designed so users can quickly and easily find everything they need. From the home screen, they have direct access to key sections such as the planner, shopping list, and recipes, while the profile section allows them to customize preferences, adjust menus, and track their progress. Everything is structured to ensure meal planning, shopping list management, and recipe discovery are seamless and efficient, keeping all key functionalities just a few clicks away.

View information architecture

User Flow

The user flow includes an initial onboarding process to personalize the experience, followed by two main options: an AI-powered generator for automated meal planning or manual recipe selection. Additional steps allow users to generate optimized shopping lists and manage their recipes intuitively.

View user flow

MVP approach to solve the problem statement

To address Planeat’s problem statement, an MVP was designed focusing on the following key areas:

  • AI-powered smart personalization to tailor meals and shopping lists to user preferences, dietary restrictions, and budget, optimizing time and savings through an assisted meal planner, automated lists, and price comparison features.
  • Seamless navigation to ensure smooth and intuitive access to all integrated features.

04. Designing with purpose

Wireframes

After the previous phases, I started designing low-fidelity wireframes to represent the structure and core functionality of Planeat. These screens reflect the MVP priorities: an onboarding and sign-up screen to configure and access the app, an assisted planner that automatically generates personalized meal plans, a shopping list assistant to manage products and compare prices, personalized recipes with details on preparation time, cost, and nutritional information, and a user profile screen to adjust preferences based on individual needs.

View wireframes

Visual Guide and Design System

After defining Planeat’s key screens, I created the visual guide and design system. I selected a color palette based on fresh, natural tones to reinforce the connection with healthy eating. The chosen typography enhances readability on mobile devices, with sizes that establish clear hierarchies to improve navigation and content comprehension. Additionally, an atomic design approach was essential in building reusable components, such as quick-action buttons integrated into shopping product cards and recipe cards, featuring key details like preparation time and cost. A fixed navigation bar was also designed to provide seamless access to main functionalities from a consistently visible location. These decisions not only ensure a cohesive visual experience but also optimize development efficiency and project scalability.

06. Testing & Validation

Usability Testing and Feedback Analysis

During usability testing with 14 users, five key tasks were evaluated, including account registration, adding a meal plan, and checking the shopping list. The tasks of "adding a meal plan" and "finding the nearest supermarket" caused the most confusion. Areas for improvement were identified in dietary goal selection, the home screen, the shopping list, and supermarket browsing, highlighting opportunities to optimize the user experience and improve clarity within the app.

View feedback collection

Key learnings & next steps

  • The biggest challenge was integrating multiple functionalities into a single app to meet users' needs.
  • Usability tests revealed confusion in critical flows, such as adding meal plans and managing shopping lists.
  • Clear navigation and simplified workflows are essential for a seamless experience in a multifunctional product.
  • High rates of misclicks and drop-offs on screens like onboarding and the shopping list indicate the need for iteration.
  • Next steps: simplify complex flows, optimize problematic screens, adjust visual elements, and validate improvements with further usability testing.
